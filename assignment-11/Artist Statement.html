My interest in art started in my teens with photography when I purchased my first 35 mm camera. I always wanted to be able to paint what I saw through the camera lens onto canvas, I began in my twenties with a drawing on the right side of the brain class which led me to painting in acrylics. Once I had my children though there wasn’t enough time in the day to follow my dream of being a artist until I was into my fifties. It was when I was forced into early retirement that I found myself registering for art and writing courses at the University of Montana and I have been enjoying every moment of this adventure, from learning ceramics to a love of printmaking. Art has been the realization of the unanswered question of life’s meaning forme. I find great satisfaction recreating nature, admiring nature and recognizing its true meaning to our survival. I believe without nature there could be no us. I have a great appreciation for wildlife's ability to survive without the comforts we as humans have become accustomed to. Along with the beauty of the outside world I see the cold harsh brutality of it that is not even questioned by animals, “It is what it is, survival of the fittest!” I want to express this to the world in my artwork. With the use of color, content, and texture in my printmaking or painting I hope to express the existence of nature’s value to our environment and existence. My work focuses on fauna and flora of the West expressing my relationship to growing up in Montana.
